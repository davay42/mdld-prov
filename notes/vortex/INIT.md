The Activity is more PROV-O aligned as the initializer, and the reasoning is worth stating precisely because it resolves a genuine epistemological question about where knowledge begins.

---

## What PROV-O Says

PROV-O is explicit on this point. An Entity does not spontaneously exist — it is brought into being by an Activity. The fundamental generative relationship is `prov:wasGeneratedBy`, which runs from Entity to Activity. An Entity without a generating Activity is an orphan — it exists in the graph but has no causal history. The Grounding shape catches exactly this: `prov:hadPrimarySource` or `prov:wasGeneratedBy` must exist on every Entity. One of those two must be the answer to "where did this come from?"

For a Goal entity, `prov:hadPrimarySource` pointing to an HTTP URL makes no sense — the Goal did not come from a web resource. It came from a person deciding to pursue something. That decision is an Activity. The Goal is what the Activity generated.

So the correct structure is:

```
Human Decision Activity
    prov:wasAssociatedWith → Human Agent
    prov:generated → Goal Entity
```

Not:

```
Goal Entity (appears from nowhere)
    prov:wasDerivedFrom → ??? 
```

---

## The Deeper Epistemological Point

This is not just a modelling convention. It reflects something true about how knowledge and intention actually work.

A Goal is not a fact about the world — it is an **intention directed at the world**. Intentions do not exist independently of the acts of intending them. A goal that nobody decided to pursue is not a goal — it is a latent possibility. The moment it becomes a goal is the moment someone chose to make it one. That choosing is an Activity. The Goal entity is the trace that Activity left in the world — its output, its commitment, its evidence.

This maps directly to the philosophy of action. In Bratman's planning theory, a goal becomes operative when an agent forms an intention and commits resources to it. The intention-formation is the event. The goal is the record of that event. PROV-O captures this precisely: the Activity is the intention-formation event, the Entity is the record.

The vortex initialises correctly when the SEED acknowledges this. The bootstrap Activity is not administrative scaffolding — it is the first real event in the provenance chain. It is the act of deciding that this knowledge tree should exist. Everything else is downstream of that act.

## What This Means for the SEED

The SEED.md is itself the output of a human act. The most honest and PROV-O-aligned SEED acknowledges this explicitly — the Goal entity is generated by the act of writing the SEED, and that act is performed by a specific agent at a specific time:

```md
[prov]  <http://www.w3.org/ns/prov#>
[vx]    <tag:project@example.com,2026:>
[p]     <tag:project@example.com,2026:p:>

# Seed Writing Activity {=vx:activity:seed .prov:Activity label}
[COMPLETE] {p:status}
[2026-03-01T09:00:00Z] {prov:startedAtTime ^^xsd:dateTime}
[2026-03-01T09:00:00Z] {prov:endedAtTime ^^xsd:dateTime}
[vx:agent:human] {=vx:agent:human ?prov:wasAssociatedWith}

{=}

# Project Goal {=vx:goal .prov:Entity label}
[Seed Writing Activity] {=vx:activity:seed !prov:generated}
[Build a reliable MDLD parser library] {p:desiredState}
```

The git commit timestamp of the SEED file is the external cryptographic anchor for that Activity's `prov:startedAtTime`. The human's git identity is the `prov:wasAssociatedWith`. The Activity and its timestamp are not literals asserted in the file — they are facts the file participates in by existing as a committed git object. The MDLD makes the relationship explicit; git makes it verifiable.

## The Recursive Closure

This resolves a question that the original vortex framing left open: what sits at the absolute centre of the figure-8, at the lowest point of the provenance chain, before anything else exists?

Not a Goal. Not a Plan. Not a Shape.

A human act of will — the decision to begin. That act is an Activity. It generates the Goal. The Goal authorises the Plan. The Plan constrains the Activities. The Activities generate the Entities. The Entities accumulate into the vault.

The vortex is not initialised by a document. It is initialised by a decision. The document is the decision's first output. This is why the vault is trustworthy at its foundation: every provenance chain, however long and however automated, terminates in a human Activity — a specific person, at a specific time, choosing to make something exist. That choice is the bedrock that no shape can generate and no agent can fake. It is the irreducible human contribution that the entire system rests on, and PROV-O is designed to make exactly that contribution explicit, attributable, and permanent.
