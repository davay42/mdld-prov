# Attribution flow
===================

## wasAttributedTo {=prov:wasAttributedTo .owl:ObjectProperty label}

Sub-property of [qualifiedInfluence] {+prov:qualifiedInfluence ?subPropertyOf}

> Attribution is the ascribing of an entity to an agent. {comment @en}

Adds an [agent] {+prov:Agent ?range} to an [entity] {+prov:Entity ?domain}.

May be qualified by [qualifiedAttribution] {+prov:qualifiedAttribution ?prov:qualifiedForm} with [Attribution] {+prov:Attribution ?prov:qualifiedForm} attached.

Inverse: [qualifiedAttribution] {prov:inverse}

## qualifiedAttribution {=prov:qualifiedAttribution .owl:ObjectProperty label}

> If this Entity prov:wasAttributedTo Agent :ag, then it can qualify how it was influenced using prov:qualifiedAttribution [ a prov:Attribution;  prov:agent :ag; :foo :bar ]. {comment @en}

As a qualified form of [wasAttributedTo] {+prov:wasAttributedTo ?prov:unqualifiedForm} it describes that an entity was [attributed] {+prov:Attribution ?range ?prov:sharesDefinitionWith} to an [entity] {+prov:Entity ?domain}.

Sub-property of [qualifiedInfluence] {+prov:qualifiedInfluence ?subPropertyOf}.

Inverse: [qualifiedAttributionOf] {prov:inverse}.

## Attribution {=prov:Attribution .Class label}

Sub-class of [AgentInfluence] {+prov:AgentInfluence ?subClassOf}

> Attribution is the ascribing of an entity to an agent.

> When an entity e is attributed to agent ag, entity e was generated by some unspecified activity that in turn was associated to agent ag. Thus, this relation is useful when the activity is not known, or irrelevant. {prov:definition @en}


> An instance of prov:Attribution provides additional descriptions about the binary prov:wasAttributedTo relation from an prov:Entity to some prov:Agent that had some responsible for it. For example, :cake prov:wasAttributedTo :baker; prov:qualifiedAttribution [ a prov:Attribution; prov:entity :baker; :foo :bar ]. {comment @en} 

==============

## Summary

The attribution flow captures the direct responsibility relationship between entities and agents, modeling how entities are ascribed to their creators or responsible parties without requiring explicit activity details. This flow answers the essential question: "Who is responsible for this entity's existence?"

Attribution represents a streamlined approach to responsibility assignment where the focus is on entity ownership rather than the specific activities that created them. This concept provides the essential mechanism for establishing intellectual property, authorship, and ownership relationships that form the foundation of creative and academic attribution systems. By enabling direct entity-to-agent relationships, PROV-O supports scenarios where activity tracking is unnecessary, unknown, or irrelevant to the accountability framework.

The wasAttributedTo relationship creates clear ownership chains that support sophisticated analysis of intellectual property, creative works, and data provenance. The bidirectional properties enable flexible querying - both "Who created this entity?" and "What entities did this agent create?" - while qualified Attribution classes enrich these relationships with contextual detail about the attribution circumstances and processes.

By providing direct entity attribution without requiring activity specification, the flow becomes essential for academic publishing, creative works management, and data governance. It enables organizations to maintain clear authorship records, support intellectual property tracking, and establish responsibility for entities even when the creation processes are unknown or irrelevant to the accountability requirements.

This streamlined approach complements more detailed activity-based provenance by providing a lightweight mechanism for establishing responsibility when the full activity context is not needed or available, making attribution the most direct path from entity to responsible agent.
